# NVIDIA RTX 5080 16GB (Blackwell, sm_120) — LXC ai-vllm, CUDA 13.2 driver

Fork binaries `prism-b9591-62061f9` (CUDA 12.8 build) — runs on a CUDA-13-only
box by borrowing the CUDA 12 runtime libs from any torch-cu128 venv:

```bash
NV=<venv>/lib/python3.12/site-packages/nvidia
export LD_LIBRARY_PATH=$PWD/bin/cuda:$NV/cuda_runtime/lib:$NV/cublas/lib
```

## Context

- Beats PrismML's published M5 Max number (66.4, and that's the 1-bit) with
  the higher-quality ternary build; ternary+dspark beats their plain H100
  number (104.8) and sits within 10% of H100+dspark (143.8).
- Effective decode bandwidth 571 GB/s of 960 (59%) — Blackwell's ALU/tensor
  resources absorb the 2-bit unpack cost that caps M1-class GPUs at 64% of a
  lower ceiling (see kernel-optimization-findings.md).
- The `p-min 0.4` tuning discovered on the T4 carries over: default dspark
  gave +37% on H100 per the whitepaper; 0.4 gives +58% here.
- Both models + drafter fit in 16GB with room for ~8K context serving
  (ternary 6.7G + drafter 1.9G ≈ 9.2G resident).
